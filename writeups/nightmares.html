Nightmares

12/26/18

Ever since the dosage on my antidepressants increased, I’ve been having vivid nightmares. During the day, the antidepressants allow me to function perfectly fine but it seems that the worst of my demons emerge at night to haunt me in my sleep.

If I had to compare my nightmares a work of fiction, they would be closer to a Greek tragedy than they are to horror film. My dreams don’t have serial killers and spiders or monsters. They’re about completely rational things like failing classes, abandonment by loved ones, and having my life ruined.

These nightmares conclude with me waking up with cold sweat and feelings of dread. My heart races and my mind wanders to all he negative things that could await me should I leave the safety of my bed. I lay there for hours.

Once I do get up though, my days are mostly stress-free. I don’t spend nearly as much time worrying about things during my waking hours as I did before the medication. Throughout the day, I am mostly able to function and take steps to improve my life and I am truly grateful for that. Perhaps you could say the meds are front-loading anxiety so that I do all my worrying at once in the nighttime. Maybe it’s better this way.
